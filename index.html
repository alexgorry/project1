U.S. General Aviation Accident and Fatalities Statistics

By Alex Gorry

Air travel has been a necessary means of transportation for quite some time now. Although flying by aircraft is generally safe, there have been a number of accidents that have lead to death over the past years. But are safety measures on planes getting better? Take a look at the data below about U.S. General Aviation to learn more about accident rates and fatalities from aviation.

(Photo: Joshua Trujillo, SeattlePI.com)

Comparing Accident Fatalities

Below are two pie charts camparing how many U.S. aviation accidents were fatal in 1975 vs 2014. Surprisingly, percentage of fatal accdients are actually higher in 2014. This could possibly be due to the increased number of planes being flown, howver, when compared side by side, there is really no difference in the prevention of fatalities from accidents.

Have Accidents Been Increasing or Decreasing?

Based on the bar graph below, it is clear that even in the last couple of years, you can see that the number of aviation accidents have decreased quite a bit. The fatal accidents have remained close the the same, however. By looking at this data, we can see that saftey precautions have definitley been improving but perhaps next there should be research conducted to understand why these accidents are still fatal, even though the accidents aren't happening as often. Additionally, when looking at the number of fatalities, in 1975 there were 1252 fatalities as compared to 2014 which had 419.
